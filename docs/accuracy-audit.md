# Report Accuracy Audit Log

**Community Technology Survey 2026 — Better Together Solutions**
**Audit conducted:** 2026-05-01
**Auditor:** Robert Smith with Claude Sonnet 4.6 (AI-assisted)
**Scope:** Part 1 report (`report_part1.md`, run ID `2026-05-01T005004Z`)
**Method:** Every quantitative claim in the generated report was verified against `quant_results.json` and raw data. Inaccuracies were identified by cross-referencing stated values and framing against computed values from the actual survey data.

---

## Summary

Ten inaccuracies were identified. Three are critical (fabricated or inverted claims); four are high-severity framing errors that misrepresent the data; three are medium-severity denominator omissions. All have been corrected in the report code and output. This log is retained as a permanent record.

---

## Inaccuracy Register

### A-001 — CRITICAL: "Strong dissatisfaction" contradicted by M=3.97

**Location:** Executive Summary, Finding #1
**Original text:** *"Strong dissatisfaction with the status quo. Respondents rate current digital tool satisfaction at an average of 3.97/5..."*
**Problem:** M=3.97 (Mdn=4.0) reflects near-moderate-to-high satisfaction, not dissatisfaction. The actual distribution: 31.3% rated 5 (very satisfied), 44.8% rated 4 (somewhat satisfied), 16.4% rated 3 (neutral), 4.5% rated 2, 3.0% rated 1. Only 7.5% expressed any dissatisfaction. The headline directly contradicts the number it cites.
**Root cause:** LLM-generated report framing interpolated a narrative about co-op desirability backwards onto the satisfaction data, inverting the finding.
**Correction:** Reframed as "Broadly satisfied, but with targeted concerns." The accurate finding is that respondents are satisfied overall but identify specific, actionable concerns — cost (37%), privacy (30%), corporate dependence (27%) — suggesting conditional satisfaction rather than dissatisfaction.

---

### A-002 — CRITICAL: "Lack of community ownership" cited as top Q6 problem — not a survey option

**Location:** Executive Summary, Finding #1
**Original text:** *"...with the most cited problems being platform fragmentation, corporate dependency, and lack of community ownership."*
**Problem:** "Lack of community ownership" does not appear anywhere in Q6 response options. The Q6 frequency table has no such option. This claim was fabricated — it describes a desideratum (Q10) not a stated problem (Q6).
**Root cause:** The LLM conflated findings from Q10 (what communities want) with Q6 (what problems exist), generating a claim not supported by any data in quant_results.json.
**Correction:** Replaced with the actual top Q6 problems: too expensive (37.3%), poor privacy (29.9%), corporate dependence (26.9%), weak support (26.9%).

### A-003 — HIGH: "Platform fragmentation" cited as a top problem — ranked 5th, not top-cited

**Location:** Executive Summary, Finding #1
**Original text:** *"...most cited problems being platform fragmentation..."*
**Problem:** "Too many tools" (the closest equivalent to "platform fragmentation") was cited by 13.4% of respondents — ranked 5th, not in the top three. The top four are: too_expensive (37.3%), poor_privacy (29.9%), corporate_dependence (26.9%), weak_support (26.9%).
**Root cause:** LLM substituted a more rhetorically appealing framing for the actual ranked data.
**Correction:** Top problems now stated from the actual frequency table in rank order.

---

### A-004 — HIGH: Skewness direction inverted

**Location:** §2.1 body text
**Original text:** *"The distribution shows a moderate positive skew (skewness = -1.049), indicating most respondents are 'somewhat satisfied' but few are 'very satisfied.'"*
**Problem:** Skewness = −1.049 is a **negative** (left) skew, not positive. A negative skew means the tail is toward *lower* values — a minority expressing dissatisfaction — while the majority clusters at the top. Additionally, the description "few are 'very satisfied'" is wrong: 31.3% (n=21) rated 5, which is the second-largest response group.
**Root cause:** The word "positive" was generated by the LLM without checking the sign of the computed value. The narrative description was then written to match the (wrong) label.
**Correction:** Correctly describes a moderate negative skew: high ratings are the norm; a small minority tail toward dissatisfaction.

---

### A-005 — HIGH: Finding #3 mentions "accessibility" twice (duplication error)

**Location:** Executive Summary, Finding #3
**Original text:** *"Accessibility & ease of use is the single most important platform feature, followed closely by long-term reliability and accessibility."*
**Problem:** "Accessibility" appears twice. The actual ranked features are: (1) Accessibility & ease of use, (2) Long-term reliability, (3) Privacy & data protection. Finding #3 should reference privacy, not accessibility again.
**Root cause:** String interpolation from `top_feature` repeated the #1 label in a sentence meant to describe #2 and #3.
**Correction:** "followed closely by long-term reliability and privacy & data protection."

---

### A-006 — HIGH: Table 1 respondent type distribution shows "_No data_"

**Location:** §1.2 Sample, Table 1
**Problem:** The `_freq_md("respondent_types")` lookup failed because the `respondent_types` key in `quant_results.json` uses sub-keys `all_responses` and `completed_only` rather than `frequency_table` or `frequency_distribution`. The table rendered as `_No data_` — an empty placeholder — in the published report.
**Root cause:** Code used a generic lookup helper that didn't account for the nested structure of the respondent_types data structure.
**Correction:** `cmd_report` now explicitly reads `quant["respondent_types"]["all_responses"]` and builds the markdown table directly.

---

### A-007 — MEDIUM: Q50 barrier percentages lack denominator statement (N=27, not N=67)

**Location:** §6 Adoption & Economic Readiness, adoption barriers table
**Problem:** Percentages (63.0%, 55.6%, etc.) are computed over the 27 respondents who answered Q50, not the full N=67. Without a stated denominator, a reader could interpret "63%" as 63% of all completed respondents. The true prevalence among all completed respondents is 17/67 = 25.4% for cost affordability.
**Root cause:** The `_freq_md()` helper renders percentages from the frequency table as-is without noting the source denominator.
**Correction:** Table header and caption now state "N=27 respondents who answered this question."

---

### A-008 — MEDIUM: Q51 support percentages lack denominator statement (N=28)

**Location:** §6 Adoption & Economic Readiness, support needed table
**Problem:** Same issue as A-007. Q51 was answered by 28 of 67 completed respondents. The 67.9% figure for "documentation" refers to 19/28, not 19/67 (which would be 28.4%).
**Correction:** Table now states N=28 denominator.

---

### A-009 — MEDIUM: Q52 price sensitivity N=28 not disclosed

**Location:** §6 Adoption & Economic Readiness, price sensitivity table
**Problem:** Q52 was answered by 28 respondents (41.8% of N=67). The table presents percentages (50.0%, 35.7%) without indicating this is a substantial subset with significant item non-response.
**Correction:** Table caption now states N=28 with a note on the 41.8% response rate for this item.

## Pattern Analysis

Of the ten inaccuracies, three share a common root cause: **the LLM-generated narrative layer interpolated rhetorical claims that were not derived from the computed data.** Specifically, findings #1 and #3 in the Executive Summary were constructed to support a pre-existing narrative about co-op desirability, and data points were substituted or invented to fit that narrative rather than reported from the source.

This is a known risk of LLM-assisted report generation and is why this audit was conducted before publication. The quantitative analysis pipeline (`cmd_quant`) is correct — all computed values in `quant_results.json` are accurate. The inaccuracies arise exclusively in the **report assembly layer** (`cmd_report`) where the LLM-generated template text makes interpretive claims.

**Mitigation going forward:**
1. Every claim in the Executive Summary must be traceable to a specific row in `quant_results.json`.
2. Skewness, kurtosis, and distributional descriptions must be derived from computed values, not narrated from labels.
3. Multi-select question percentages must always state their denominator explicitly.
4. Cross-question claims (using findings from Q-X to describe Q-Y) require explicit traceability.

## Addendum — 2026-05-01: Incomplete dataset used in original run

### A-010 — CRITICAL: Original pipeline run used an incomplete export (N=112 instead of N=137)

**Discovered:** 2026-05-01, during correction of the collection period dates.
**Original run ID:** `2026-05-01T005004Z`
**Corrected run ID:** `2026-05-01T032821Z`

**Problem:** The pipeline auto-detected `data/original/export-community_technology_survey-2026-04-14-23-01-37.xlsx` as the latest export. This file contained only the responses collected through April 14, 2026. The full dataset — covering the complete collection period through April 30, 2026 — existed on `bts-lt-0` (the analyst's offsite laptop) and had not yet been copied to the analysis workstation.

**Impact on statistics:**

| Metric | Incomplete run (N=112/67) | Correct run (N=137/80) |
|--------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| Total responses | 112 | **137** |
| Completed responses | 67 | **80** |
| Completion rate | 59.8% | **58.4%** |
| Q5 satisfaction mean | 3.97 | **3.80** |
| Q5 SD | 0.969 | **1.06** |
| Q10 ownership (very+extremely important) | 70% | **68%** |
| Q11 trust increase | 81% | **81%** |
| Q6 #1 problem | too_expensive (37.3%) | **too_expensive (37.5%)** |
| Q6 #2 problem | poor_privacy (29.9%) | **corporate_dependence (31.2%)** |
| Q7 top feature | Accessibility & ease of use (4.714) | **Accessibility & ease of use (4.605)** |

**Key finding:** The directional conclusions are consistent across both runs. Trust and adoption metrics (Q11 81%, Q12 ~85%) are essentially unchanged. The Q5 mean drops slightly (3.97 → 3.80), remaining in the "moderately satisfied" range — the corrected framing from A-001 holds. Q6 rank order shifts slightly: corporate dependence rises to #2, with poor privacy at #3.

**Root cause:** The full export was only available on the analyst's local device (`bts-lt-0`) and had not been synced to the shared repository before the pipeline ran. The fallback auto-detection logic correctly picked the most recent *available* file, but that file was not the final export.

**Resolution:** Full export (`export-community_technology_survey-2026-05-01-00-08-26.xlsx`, 137 rows, March 27 – April 30 2026) copied from `bts-lt-0` via SSH/Headscale and added to `data/original/`. Pipeline re-run as `2026-05-01T032821Z`. All published outputs updated from the corrected run.

**Collection period (corrected):** March 27, 2026 – April 30, 2026 (35 days).
